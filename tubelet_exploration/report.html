V-JEPA 2.1 Tubelet Tokenizer & Attention Tracking

Exploring how V-JEPA 2.1 partitions video spatio-temporal grids into 3D tubelets and tracks attention propagation from high-motion query tokens across transformer depths.

📦 Understanding 3D Tubelets

Unlike standard Vision Transformers (ViTs) that treat video frames as independent images, V-JEPA 2.1 utilizes a 3D Tubelet Tokenizer. It maps spatial-temporal cylinders (tubelets) of raw video pixels into distinct token representations.

Tokenizer Geometry:
- Spatial Patch Size (H × W): 16 × 16 pixels.
- Temporal Tubelet Size (T): 2 frames.
- Convolution Projector: nn.Conv3d with kernel and stride (2, 16, 16).
- This means a video of shape [16 frames, 256px, 256px] is compressed into 8 × 16 × 16 = 2048 tubelet tokens.

🔎 Layer-wise Attention Propagation

To understand how V-JEPA 2.1 integrates visual structures, we selected a high-motion query tubelet (the bowler's arm region at t=7, y=13, x=7) and tracked where its attention propagates at different layers:

  • Layer 2 (Early local grouping): The attention heatmap is extremely localized around the query token itself and its immediate spatial neighbors. The temporal context is ignored, validating that early layers establish spatial boundaries first.
  • Layer 8 (Spatio-Temporal Tracking): Attention starts tracking the temporal trajectory. The attention spreads backwards in time, aligning with the path of the bowler's movement. It selectively ignores the static bowling lane background.
  • Layer 14 (Global Object Grouping): Attention integrates the entire silhouette of the bowler across the entire duration of the clip. The network binds the moving bowler together as a unified semantic object.
  • Layer 20 (Disentanglement / Mask prediction): The attention pattern shifts from simple tracking to a semantic partition mask. The model isolates the background structure of the lane and pins while maintaining focus on the bowler's action, preparing representations for target reconstruction.
